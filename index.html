About me

I am a 3rd year graduate student at UCSD, working under Kiran Kedlaya and Aaron Pollack. I expect to graduate in 2027. I'm interested in number theory, arithmetic geometry, homotopy theory and everything in between.

What I'm thinking about

  • Almost all current methods for finding rational points on modular curves involve first finding a plane model before implementing quadratic Chabauty. With Kiran Kedlaya, I am currently developing a way to work directly with the moduli space without using any defining equations. Expect a draft to appear "soon".
  • With Aaron Pollack, I am investigating relations between cohomology classes of certain special cycles on the symmetric space associated to the split real form of G_2. Investigating these relations involves producing half-integral weight modular forms via theta lifting. Expect a draft to appear "soon".
  • To show that BB(5) = 47,176,870, there are only a few holdout 5-state 2-symbol Turing machines to check that do not halt. My paper below tackles arguably the most difficult of the holdouts.
